Research workflow

1
Identify heat clusters on Strava (bike + trail run filters)
Look for bright convergence zones outside major metro areas. These are athlete magnets with existing infrastructure.
2
Validate trail quality on Trailforks
Check ridelog counts per trail, star ratings, trail status (open/closed), and whether the network is expanding. Avoid stagnant networks.
3
Check AllTrails for trail running + hiker volume
High review counts + difficulty distribution (moderate to hard) signal the right demographic. Look for towns with 500+ reviews per major trail.
4
Map events: races, gran fondos, enduro series
Recurring events (XTERRA, EWS, NUE, Dirty Kanza-style gravel) create reliable shoulder-season demand. Check mtbracenews.com, USA Cycling calendar.
5
Pull land prices via LandWatch / Lands of America / Idealista (Spain)
Target: within 10 miles of trail head, 20–100 acres, rural zoning. Sweet spot is where land is still <$10k/acre US or <€15k/ha Spain but trail activity is already dense.
6
Score on 4 axes: trail density, land cost, competitor gap, year-round viability
Avoid: known resort towns (Moab, Sedona) — land is priced in. Target: the next Bentonville or next Ainsa.
7
Check campground supply vs demand
Use The Dyrt, Hipcamp, and Recreation.gov — search the area and see how quickly sites book out. Fully booked weekends = demand signal.

Land price context (USD thousands/acre equiv.)

Resort towns (Moab, Sedona, Park City) command premiums of 3–10× adjacent rural land because they're already "discovered." The opportunity is the 5–10 mile radius outside established trail towns where land hasn't yet re-priced but demand is growing.

Spain opportunity context

Spain's rural interior ("España vacía") is actively incentivizing tourism development to combat depopulation. Regions like Aragón, Castilla-La Mancha, and Extremadura offer land from €5–15k/ha near world-class MTB and trail running terrain. The Pyrenees (Ainsa, Benasque) and Sierra Nevada (La Alpujarra) regions are seeing growing international athlete tourism with under-supplied quality accommodation.
